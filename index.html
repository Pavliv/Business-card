Flight of the eagle

They have at least one singular characteristic. It has been observed that most birds of prey look back over their shoulders before striking prey (or shortly thereafter); predation is after all a two-edged sword. All hawks seem to have this habit, from the smallest kestrel to the largest Ferruginous - but not the Eagles.

Opportunities

    • food

    Sea eagles or fish eagles take fish as a large part of their diets, either fresh or as carrion. Snake or serpent eagles are, as the name suggests, adapted to hunting reptiles.

    • high speed

    There are cases where an eagle in flight speed reached 130 km/h. Even the smallest eagles have relatively long and wide wings, and capable of rapid flight.

    • keen eyesight

    They have extremely keen eyesight that allows them to find prey at a distance. This vision eagles are primarily due to the extremely large pupils which ensure minimal diffraction of light.

    • nidification

    Over the years, Eagle Nest increases and can reach 2 meters tall and weigh 1 ton. Eagles build their nests in tall trees or on high cliffs.
